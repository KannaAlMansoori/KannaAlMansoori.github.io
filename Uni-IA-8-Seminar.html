Unit 8 Seminar Preparation

Understanding Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Status: Preparation in progress | Seminar: Not yet attended

What's This About?

This seminar is about NLP - how computers understand human language.

It's practical: we'll see a demo and build some NLP structures ourselves.

Join the Seminar

What I Already Know

From Agent Communication (CD2)

Agents communicate using formal languages like KQML. Everything is explicit and structured. No ambiguity.

NLP is the opposite. Humans speak naturally. Words are ambiguous. Context matters. Computers have to figure out what we mean.

So NLP is about turning messy human language into something structured that machines can work with.

What to Focus On

1. How Do Computers Break Down Language?

Watch the demo. What steps does the system take to understand a sentence?

2. How Do Computers Extract Meaning?

It's one thing to parse grammar. It's another to understand what something actually means.

3. What Structures Will We Build?

The seminar will show actual NLP structures. Understand what they do.

4. Where Is This Used?

Chatbots, translation, search engines. Why does NLP matter in real systems?
