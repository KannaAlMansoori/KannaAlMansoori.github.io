Module Aims and Learning Outcomes

In this module, we shall acquire:

  • Develop a systematic understanding of foundational mathematical principles and methods, as well as core and specialised concepts underpinning computing logic.
  • Understand the foundation for the development and application of programming and data-driven techniques, from both a theoretical and practical viewpoint.
  • Facilitate the ability to interpret the results generated when using these data science and AI tools.
  • Gain an understanding of the real-world applications of these computational tools, and contemporary issues related to these computational techniques.
  • The opportunity to take a reflective and independent approach to the learning process.

On completion of this module, you will be able to:

  1. Demonstrate systematic understanding of the key mathematical and statistical concepts and techniques which underpin mechanisms in Data Science and AI.
  2. Apply mathematical and statistical methods in these fields to help in the decision-making process.
  3. Critically evaluate the use of statistical analysis and the numeric interpretation of results as aids in the decision-making process.
  4. Critically appraise and present results of a statistical analysis to a diverse audience.

Initial exploration of COVID-19 India dataset (January-March 2020) using R. Tasks included determining dataset structure, variable types, and extracting key information about states and date ranges.

Unit 2: Seminar Preparation

Title: Understanding Descriptive Statistics | Deadline: End of unit 2 | Type: Formative | Status: ✓ Complete

Task Overview:

Read and analyze the Dong (2023) paper on descriptive statistics applications. Answered seminar questions covering the purpose, limitations, and practical applications of descriptive statistics. Completed dataset activity with COVID-19 data including measures of central tendency and various visualizations.

Key Learning:

Gained comprehensive understanding of descriptive statistics fundamentals and their real-world applications. Learned to calculate and interpret mean, median, mode, and create statistical visualizations including histograms, boxplots, and violin plots.

Unit 2: Data Activity 2 - Frequency Analysis

Deadline: End of Unit 2 | Type: Formative | Status: ✓ Complete

Task Overview:

Frequency analysis of COVID-19 India dataset (January-March 2020) focusing on recovery reporting patterns. Created binary variables, generated frequency tables, and calculated percentages to understand the proportion of daily state reports that included recovery cases.

Key Learning:

Mastered frequency distribution analysis using R's table() and prop.table() functions. Discovered that 79.63% of reports showed no recoveries while only 20.37% reported recoveries, revealing important insights about early pandemic dynamics.

Unit 3: Data Activity 3 - Categorical Variables

Deadline: End of Unit 3 | Type: Formative | Status: ✓ Complete

Task Overview:

Advanced categorical variable creation including binary death indicators, multi-level case severity classification, and geographic distribution analysis. Created has_deaths and case_level variables, analyzed state-level reporting patterns, and identified top 10 states with most frequent COVID-19 reports.

Key Learning:

Mastered factor conversion and the cut() function for multi-level categorization. Discovered that 96.67% of reports had no deaths, 52.96% showed low case counts, and Kerala led with 52 reports—nearly 20% of all observations during January-March 2020.
