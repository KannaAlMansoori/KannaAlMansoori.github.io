Intelligent Agents

Period: Ongoing | Status:Complete

This module explores one of the most exciting and rapidly evolving fields within computing: Intelligent Agents. Agents are software programs that can understand their environment and act in a way that helps them achieve their desired goals. They are an intelligent and innovative tool that can be deployed across a range of scenarios in both the private and public sectors.

Module Overview

In this module, students gain critical insight into the theory, tools and techniques of intelligent agents, dealing with both individual agents and their interactions. Through a systematic approach, students develop essential knowledge to design these systems using applicable programming languages such as Java and Python, while applying knowledge and skills from previous modules.

The module covers state-of-the-art models and the latest developments in model deployment. For coding exercises, students are encouraged to create GitHub repositories to save their work and support team projects, with repository links featured in the e-portfolio.

Module Aims and Learning Outcomes

Module Aims:

  • Develop an understanding of the motivations for, and appropriate use of, agent-based computing.
  • Understand the main agent models in use today and their grounding in artificial intelligence research.
  • Acquire the knowledge and skills required to develop, deploy and evaluate intelligent systems to solve real-world problems.
  • Understand contemporary research issues in the area of intelligent agent systems.

On Completion, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Identify and critically analyse agent-based systems, differentiating between architectures and approaches.
  2. Apply and critically evaluate intelligent agent techniques to real-world problems, particularly where technical risk and uncertainty is involved.
  3. Deploy critically appropriate software tools and skills for the design and implementation of an agent-based system, bearing in mind applicable legal, social, ethical and professional issues.
  4. Systematically develop and implement the skills required to be an effective member of a development team in a virtual professional environment, adopting real-life perspectives on team roles and organisation.

Assessment Overview

This module incorporates both formative and summative assessment through an e-portfolio. Regular formative assessment is provided via case studies, exercises, and reflective commentaries. The formative feedback enables development of understanding required for summative assessments.

Recommended: Participate in all formative activities and make frequent entries to your e-portfolio. Regular engagement with these activities will help you gain a deep understanding of module content and provide a strong foundation for the summative assessments.
