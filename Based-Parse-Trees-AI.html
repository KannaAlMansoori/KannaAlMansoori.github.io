Activity: Constituency-based parse trees

The three sentences below were parsed using phrase-structure grammar, in which each sentence (S) divides into a subject noun phrase (NP) and a predicate verb phrase (VP), following the core rule S → NP VP. The completed parse trees are shown below, with a summary of each.

1. The government raised interest rates.

A straightforward S → NP VP structure. The subject NP is "The government" (Det + N), and the VP is "raised" plus the object NP "interest rates". Because "interest rates" is a compound noun, "interest" sits inside the object NP as a modifier of "rates".

2. The internet gives everyone a voice.

"gives" is a ditransitive verb, so the VP branches into two noun phrases: the indirect object "everyone" and the direct object "a voice". This shows how the structure of the verb phrase depends on the verb's argument requirements.

3. The man saw the dog with the telescope.

This sentence is deliberately ambiguous and produces two valid trees:

Reading A — telescope is the instrument (PP under VP)

The prepositional phrase "with the telescope" attaches to the VP — the man used the telescope to see the dog.

Reading B — the dog has the telescope (PP under NP)

The PP attaches to the NP "the dog" — the dog is the one holding the telescope. This is a classic example of PP-attachment ambiguity, where a single string of words yields two grammatical structures and two meanings.

Link to the learning outcomes

Constituency parsing is a fundamental technique in natural language processing, which is how intelligent agents interpret human language — addressing the outcome on applying the tools and techniques of intelligent systems to real-world problems. Sentence 3's ambiguity connects to contemporary research in intelligent agent systems: a parser cannot resolve "with the telescope" from grammar alone, so modern systems rely on probabilistic parsing and contextual cues to select the most likely structure. Resolving this kind of structural ambiguity remains an active challenge in NLP and language understanding.
